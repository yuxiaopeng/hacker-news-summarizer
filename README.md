## 1. 手写对大脑有益。

**原文标题**: Writing by hand is good for your brain

**原文链接**: [https://nealstephenson.substack.com/p/writing-by-hand-is-good-for-your](https://nealstephenson.substack.com/p/writing-by-hand-is-good-for-your)

In this article, novelist Neal Stephenson explores the cognitive and creative advantages of writing by hand, a practice he has maintained for most of his career. He anchors his argument in recent neuroscience research—specifically a study from the Norwegian University of Science and Technology—which suggests that the intricate movements involved in handwriting stimulate brain patterns essential for learning and memory in ways that typing does not.

Stephenson outlines several key reasons why handwriting remains his preferred method for first drafts:

*   **Cognitive Engagement:** The physical act of forming letters forces the brain to process information more deeply. Unlike the mechanical, repetitive motion of typing, handwriting creates unique neural pathways that aid in better retention and conceptual understanding.
*   **Creative Flow vs. Editing:** Typing makes it too easy to delete and rearrange text, often leading to "premature editing." Handwriting, by contrast, encourages a forward-moving creative flow because it is more difficult to erase or over-analyze the prose in the moment.
*   **The "Second Pass" Benefit:** Stephenson views the transition from paper to screen as a critical part of his process. By typing up his handwritten notes later, he performs a natural first edit, allowing him to refine the story with a fresh perspective.
*   **Tactile Experience:** He highlights the personal satisfaction found in using high-quality tools, such as fountain pens and specific types of paper, which makes the labor-intensive process of novel-writing more aesthetically pleasing.

Ultimately, Stephenson argues that while digital tools offer speed and efficiency, the "analog" method of handwriting fosters a deeper connection between the hand and the brain, resulting in more thoughtful and structured creative work.

## 6. 天文学家或已发现首颗系外卫星

**原文标题**: Astronomers may have found the first exomoon

**原文链接**: [https://www.eso.org/public/news/eso2610/](https://www.eso.org/public/news/eso2610/)

使用欧洲南方天文台甚大望远镜（VLT）的天文学家宣布，在 CD-35 2722 系统中发现了一个“类月”天体，这可能标志着首个获得证实的系外卫星发现。

该系统的结构非常独特，挑战了传统的天体分类。其中心是一颗质量为太阳一半的恒星，由一颗棕矮星（质量是木星的30倍）环绕运行。而新发现的“系外卫星”绕着这颗棕矮星运行，而非绕着传统的行星运行。虽然它的职能类似于卫星，但该天体是一颗质量至少与木星相当的气态巨行星，模糊了行星与卫星之间的界限。

论文第一作者凯文·霍伊（Kevin Hoy）及其团队利用 VLT 上的 CRIRES+ 仪器识别出了该天体。他们采用了视向速度法，即通过探测轨道天体对其宿主（在此案例中为棕矮星）施加的微小引力“摆动”来进行观测。

这一发现意义重大，因为尽管目前已发现 6,000 多颗系外行星，但确凿的系外卫星一直难以寻获。这项发表在《自然》（Nature）杂志上的研究结果表明，行星系统的多样性可能远超我们的太阳系。

展望未来，研究人员预计欧洲南方天文台即将建成的极大望远镜（ELT）将能够探测到更小的岩石系外卫星。此类发现有望进一步彻底改变我们对天体形成方式的理解，以及在非类太阳系系统中对天体进行分类的方式。

## 7. 软件工厂为何失败（或者：仅靠工程化是不够的）

**原文标题**: Why Software Factories Fail (or: harness engineering is not enough)

**原文链接**: [https://github.com/humanlayer/advanced-context-engineering-for-coding-agents/blob/main/wsff.md](https://github.com/humanlayer/advanced-context-engineering-for-coding-agents/blob/main/wsff.md)

In "Why Software Factories Fail," Dex argues that the industry’s rush toward "lights-off" software factories—where AI agents generate, test, and deploy code without human oversight—is fundamentally flawed. While the prevailing narrative promises 100x velocity by removing human bottlenecks, evidence suggests this approach leads to declining PR quality, increased production incidents, and rapid "codebase rot."

Dex challenges the notion that these failures are merely "skill issues" or solvable through better "harness engineering" (e.g., adding more linters or automated review loops). Instead, he identifies a fundamental model-training problem: current AI models excel at solving one-off tasks but lack the ability to maintain long-term architectural health and maintainability. This results in "shotgun surgery" and "spaghetti code" that eventually requires manual, time-consuming rewrites once the system becomes too complex for the agent to navigate.

A critical distinction is made between "vibe coding" (small-scale, disposable projects) and maintaining complex enterprise systems. In the latter, the historical practice of "front-loading alignment"—planning and architectural oversight—remains essential. The article notes that while tools like Claude Code have improved agentic performance by using Reinforcement Learning (RL) within the tool harness itself, there are still no reliable benchmarks for long-term codebase maintainability. 

Ultimately, the author concludes that "token-maxxing" and automated loops cannot replace human steering. To prevent software from falling apart, engineers must remain involved in high-level alignment and code quality, as no amount of harness engineering can fix the limitations of the underlying models.
