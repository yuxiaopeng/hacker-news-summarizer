## 2. 丁腈和乳胶手套可能导致微塑料的高估

**原文标题**: Nitrile and latex gloves may cause overestimation of microplastics

**原文链接**: [https://news.umich.edu/nitrile-and-latex-gloves-may-cause-overestimation-of-microplastics-u-m-study-reveals/](https://news.umich.edu/nitrile-and-latex-gloves-may-cause-overestimation-of-microplastics-u-m-study-reveals/)

A study led by the University of Michigan reveals that nitrile and latex gloves—the standard personal protective equipment used in scientific research—are a significant source of micro- and nanoplastic contamination. This finding suggests that many previous studies may have overestimated the concentration of microplastics in environmental and food samples due to background interference from the researchers' own gear.

**Key Findings:**
*   **Shedding Mechanism:** The research, led by Dušan Materić, found that the friction generated by putting on gloves or moving one's hands during lab work causes the materials to shed microscopic particles. 
*   **Significant Volume:** The study quantified this shedding, noting that simply putting on a pair of nitrile gloves can release hundreds of particles. Over an hour of typical laboratory activity, thousands of micro- and nanoplastic fragments can be shed into the surrounding environment and samples.
*   **Data Integrity:** Because these particles are often the same materials being studied (like synthetic polymers), they are difficult to distinguish from the actual environmental pollutants. This likely led to "false positives" or inflated counts in earlier research regarding microplastics in water, air, and soil.

**Recommendations:**
To address this issue, the researchers emphasize the need for stricter laboratory protocols. They suggest that scientists must:
1.  **Use "Blanks":** Consistently run control samples to measure and subtract the background contamination caused by gloves.
2.  **Refine Techniques:** Explore alternative glove materials or minimize hand-over-sample movements to reduce shedding.

Ultimately, the study serves as a critical call for the scientific community to refine sampling and analytical techniques to ensure the accuracy of future microplastic research.

## 4. Miasma：一款将 AI 网页爬虫困在无尽毒坑中的工具。

**原文标题**: Miasma: A tool to trap AI web scrapers in an endless poison pit

**原文链接**: [https://github.com/austin-weeks/miasma](https://github.com/austin-weeks/miasma)

**Miasma** 是一款资源高效型工具，旨在通过将机器人困在“无尽的毒坑”中，打击未经授权的 AI 网页抓取行为。该工具为保护知识产权而开发，通过自引用链接的无限循环，向抓取工具提供低质量的“中毒”训练数据，从而有效浪费抓取者的计算资源并污染其数据集。

**关键机制与实现：**
*   **陷阱：** 网站所有者嵌入不可见的 HTML 链接（通过 CSS 对人类隐藏），这些链接指向特定目录，例如 `/bots`。
*   **路由：** 利用 Nginx 等反向代理，将访问该目录的流量路由至 Miasma 服务器。
*   **数据交付：** 一旦掉入陷阱，机器人将接收到“垃圾（slop）”数据和多个导回同一系统的链接，形成源源不断的垃圾数据大餐。

**技术特性：**
*   **高效：** Miasma 使用 Rust (Cargo) 构建，旨在实现极速运行和极低内存占用。用户可以限制“在途（in-flight）”请求数以管理资源使用。
*   **配置：** 用户可以自定义链接前缀、每页链接数以及中毒数据的来源。它还支持强制 gzip 压缩，以最大限度地降低流出带宽成本。
*   **安全：** 文档强调通过在网站的 `robots.txt` 文件中明确排除陷阱目录，来保护“友好”的机器人（如 Googlebot 或 Bingbot）。

总之，Miasma 为网站所有者提供了一种主动防御手段，在干扰 AI 公司数据采集工作的同时，确保主机服务器保持较低的开销。

## 6. IBM 4 Pi 航空航天计算机的兴衰：图说历史

**原文标题**: The rise and fall of IBM's 4 Pi aerospace computers: an illustrated history

**原文链接**: [https://www.righto.com/2026/03/ibm-4-pi-computer-history.html](https://www.righto.com/2026/03/ibm-4-pi-computer-history.html)

IBM’s System/4 Pi family, introduced in 1967, was a revolutionary line of aerospace computers that powered critical military and space missions for decades. Named to represent a "full sphere" of 3D applications (4π steradians), the series adapted the architecture of IBM’s System/360 mainframes for the compact, rugged requirements of avionics.

The first generation included three primary models:
*   **TC (Tactical Computer):** A lightweight, low-cost unit used for tactical missiles and Skylab, where it powered the first fully digital control system on a crewed spacecraft.
*   **CP (Customized Processor):** Designed for real-time I/O, it was used in aircraft like the EA-6B and F-111, though early versions faced some reliability challenges.
*   **EP (Extended Performance):** A 32-bit, System/360-compatible computer intended for high-data applications like the Manned Orbiting Laboratory.

In the 1970s, IBM introduced the **Advanced System/4 Pi**, utilizing Medium-Scale Integration (MSI) and faster core memory. The most prominent model was the **AP (Advanced Processor)**. The **AP-1** and **AP-101** became staples of military and research aviation, appearing in the F-15 fighter and NASA’s fly-by-wire programs. 

The family’s most famous achievement was the **AP-101B**, which controlled the Space Shuttle. To ensure safety, the Shuttle utilized four redundant AP-101B units running in parallel, with a fifth backup computer running independent software. 

Despite their historical importance in everything from B-52 bombers to the development of GPS, the 4 Pi series remains an under-documented chapter of computing history. These machines bridged the gap between room-sized mainframes and modern flight controllers, proving that digital computers could handle the rigors of spaceflight and combat.

## 20. Siclair Microvision (1977)

**原文标题**: Siclair Microvision (1977)

**原文链接**: [https://r-type.org/articles/art-452.htm](https://r-type.org/articles/art-452.htm)

In March 1977, Clive Sinclair officially launched the Sinclair Microvision, a revolutionary portable television featuring a 2-inch screen. Developed over 14 years at a cost of £500,000, the device was marketed as a high-end "prestige" tool for international business travelers rather than a toy, carrying a significant price tag of £175 (plus VAT).

Technically, the Microvision was a feat of miniaturization and efficiency. Measuring 6 x 4 x 1½ inches, it utilized a custom-designed AEG Telefunken tube and five integrated circuits. To maximize battery life, it employed electrostatic deflection, resulting in an impressively low power consumption of 750mW. The set was uniquely versatile, supporting multiple international standards (625/525 lines) and various IF frequencies, allowing it to function globally. 

Despite its innovation, the launch was met with professional skepticism. Critics questioned the practicality of the tiny screen, though Sinclair argued the viewing experience was comparable to a 24-inch set when held at a natural reading distance. Furthermore, the press remained cynical regarding production timelines. Despite Sinclair’s insistence that the product would be in shops within a month, he remained secretive about production volumes and manufacturing specifics.

While Sinclair envisioned the Microvision eventually reaching a mass market similar to pocket calculators, the initial model was positioned as a luxury item. Sinclair dismissed the possibility of a color version in the near future, citing technological hurdles and prohibitive costs, choosing instead to focus on his "monopoly" in the miniature black-and-white market.
