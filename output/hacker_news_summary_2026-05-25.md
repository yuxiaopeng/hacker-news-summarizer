## 4. 加州在遭到反对后，拟豁免 Linux 遵守其年龄验证法。

**原文标题**: California moves to exempt Linux from its age-verification law after backlash

**原文链接**: [https://www.tomshardware.com/software/linux/california-moves-to-exempt-linux-from-its-upcoming-age-verification-law-after-backlash-over-forcing-operating-systems-to-collect-users-ages-amendment-proposed-by-the-same-lawmaker-who-wrote-the-original-law](https://www.tomshardware.com/software/linux/california-moves-to-exempt-linux-from-its-upcoming-age-verification-law-after-backlash-over-forcing-operating-systems-to-collect-users-ages-amendment-proposed-by-the-same-lawmaker-who-wrote-the-original-law)

加州立法者提出了第 1856 号议会法案（AB 1856），旨在豁免大多数开源操作系统，使其不受该州即将实施的《数字年龄确认法案》（AB 1043）的约束。此举是在科技界和隐私倡导者的强烈抵制之后做出的。

原法律定于 2027 年生效，要求操作系统在设备设置期间验证用户年龄，并将该数据同步至应用程序和商店。包括电子前哨基金会（EFF）在内的批评人士认为，这对于像 Debian 或 Mint 这样去中心化、由社区运行的 Linux 发行版来说，在技术上是不可行的，且会为侵入性身份追踪构建危险的基础设施。

拟议的修正案（AB 1856）缩小了“操作系统提供商”的定义，排除了那些根据允许用户**复制、重新分发和修改**代码的许可协议来分发软件的实体。这有效地保护了缺乏集中式年龄验证所需的各种企业架构或遥测系统的核心主流开源项目。

要点包括：
*   **定向豁免：** 大多数由社区主导的 Linux 发行版将获得豁免。
*   **商业影响：** 拥有集成应用生态系统的专有平台和商业操作系统提供商（如苹果和谷歌）仍需遵守该法律。 
*   **模糊状态：** 对于像 Valve 的 SteamOS 这样虽然使用 Linux 但与专有商店绑定的平台，目前仍存在不确定性。
*   **立法进展：** 该修正案由议员 Buffy Wicks 提出，目前正在立法机构审议中，最近一次审议是在 2026 年 5 月。

该修正案代表了一种折衷方案，既维持了该州在主要商业平台上保护未成年人的目标，又避免了监管去中心化开源软件所带来的技术和法律挑战。

## 8. IBM拆分出首家纯量子芯片代工厂

**原文标题**: IBM Spins Off the First Pure-Play Quantum Chip Foundry

**原文链接**: [https://futurumgroup.com/insights/2-billion-chips-act-investment-in-quantum-bets-on-ibms-300mm-superconducting-silicon/](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/2-billion-chips-act-investment-in-quantum-bets-on-ibms-300mm-superconducting-silicon/)

IBM and the U.S. Department of Commerce have announced the creation of **Anderon**, the first pure-play quantum chip foundry in the United States. Located in Albany, New York, the $2 billion initiative is funded by a $1 billion CHIPS Act grant and $1 billion from IBM. 

Anderon represents a significant shift in U.S. industrial policy, concentrating major manufacturing capital into **300mm superconducting silicon**. This modality was chosen for its ability to leverage existing semiconductor fabrication infrastructure, offering a 30x throughput advantage over the 200mm environments used for research. While the government distributed a broader $2 billion package across nine companies—including GlobalFoundries, D-Wave, and Rigetti—the $1 billion allocated to Anderon establishes it as the centerpiece of the American quantum ecosystem.

Key strategic points include:
*   **Manufacturing Hierarchy:** The funding creates a two-tier system, prioritizing superconducting silicon for production-scale infrastructure while providing smaller equity-based investments for alternative modalities like trapped-ion and photonic systems.
*   **System Integration:** Anderon will produce both qubit wafers and custom **ASIC control architectures**. IBM is developing four specialized ASICs to manage power and scaling, targeting fault-tolerant quantum computing by 2029.
*   **Government Involvement:** The Department of Commerce will take minority equity stakes in the nine funded companies, a deal structure similar to previous investments in Intel and strategic mining.

The initiative aims to transition quantum technology from laboratory prototypes to mass-manufactured products. However, the concentration of capital raises questions about whether backing one dominant modality will accelerate U.S. leadership or create fragility by underinvesting in alternative architectures.

## 9. Gnutella：一个比创造它的世界更长久的协议

**原文标题**: Gnutella: A Protocol Outliving the World That Created It

**原文链接**: [https://rickcarlino.com/notes/p2p/gnutella-explanation.html](https://rickcarlino.com/notes/p2p/gnutella-explanation.html)

**Summary of "Gnutella: A Protocol Outliving the World That Created It"**

Gnutella is a resilient, decentralized file-sharing protocol that defined the early 2000s internet. Originally developed at AOL and leaked after the project’s cancellation, its server-free architecture made it impossible to shut down. While often dismissed as a "failed" technology, the author argues that Gnutella successfully scaled to millions of users, thriving through clients like LimeWire until the transition from dial-up to streaming changed consumer habits.

Technically, Gnutella operates as a peer-to-peer search engine for data "blobs." Nodes function by hosting local HTTP servers for file transfers while participating in a TCP-based gossip mesh to search for content. Because there is no central registry, new users join through "bootstrapping"—utilizing a federation of GWebCache servers to discover initial peer IP addresses. Once connected, nodes exchange five core binary messages:
*   **PING/PONG:** For peer discovery and network mapping.
*   **QUERY/QUERYHIT:** For searching and receiving file results.
*   **PUSH:** For bypassing firewalls by requesting the uploader connect to the downloader.

The protocol’s longevity is attributed to its simplicity and extensibility. Through frameworks like the Gnutella Generic Extension Protocol (GGEP) and Hash/URN Gnutella Extensions (HUGE), developers were able to add features like SHA hashing and TLS without breaking the core spec.

Ultimately, Gnutella represents a "pure" decentralized technology that succeeded because it solved a real-world problem (accessing MP3s) without speculative incentives. Though it has faded from the mainstream, the network remains operational—a "long tail" survivor and a testament to the durability of interoperable, serverless software.
